Customization for the Audience

I framed this for mixed audiences - clear “why it matters” for non-technical readers, actionable levers (governance, A/B tests, MLOps costs)
for technical peers, and outcome framing (OKRs, adoption metrics) for leaders.

Lessons Learned

My strengths are ethical, explainable AI and cross-role communication that links tech to business value. I’m growing in ROI/budgeting,
role-based training so adoption sticks, and measuring impact with disciplined experiments and KPIs.

Feedback & Revisions

The tools were helpful but broad. I’d add model cards and data lineage, privacy/security-by-design, scenario-based items (drift, bias, resistance),
and scale anchors - plus track time-to-proficiency, utilization, and sentiment/NPS.

Lessons Learned (On Rechecking)

I learned the hard way that small details - like a page title - scan undo solid work if I don’t double-check. Going forward, I’ll run a quick
pre-submit checklist (titles, links, rubric alignment) to catch simple errors before they cause confusion. This tiny habit protects clarity and ensures my work
is judged on its substance, not its metadata.
