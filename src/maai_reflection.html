Reflection on Ma.ai


Customization for the Audience
I wrote this artifact for instructors and hiring managers. The page leads with plain-language sections (Description, Objective, Process, Tools) and emphasizes safety and clarity over technical depth, while the prototype and evaluation details are available for those who want to explore further.
Lessons Learned
Narrowing scope (age/stage, clear intake, crisis pathway) dramatically improved answer quality and reduced risk from generic LLM advice. A lightweight Streamlit prototype made it easy to iterate on tone and next steps, and a side-by-side comparison across Gemini, Copilot, and ChatGPT taught me to assess responses on warmth, thoroughness, clarity, and safety—not just correctness.
Feedback & Revisions
Peer feedback flagged jargon and dense text; I simplified headings, tightened copy, and improved spacing. I clarified what the bot will and won’t do (no diagnosis), surfaced trusted-source framing, and made the crisis mode more prominent. I also listed concrete tools/tech and captured decisions in a brief log to make the work reproducible and auditable.
