Reflection on Learning as Worship


Customization for the Audience

I wrote the brief for two groups: instructors, who need to see critical reflection across theology and tech, and hiring managers,
who need a clear, practical stance on how I’ll lead teams that use AI. The portfolio page is concise and non-technical;
the downloadable brief carries the full argument and citations.

Lessons Learned

Linking neuroplasticity to Imago Dei reframed learning as formation, not just skill acquisition.
The Moses/Esther cases moved me from self-reliance to dependent, courageous leadership under uncertainty.
The communication assignment convicted me that clarity is not only a productivity tool but a spiritual discipline that guards unity and trust.
Together, these insights clarify what AI can assist with (pattern detection, summarization, speed) and
what it cannot replace: moral agency, purpose, empathy, and worshipful reasoning.

Feedback & Revisions

Feedback across earlier assignments emphasized:

(1) be explicit about how faith informs action,
(2) make the leadership lessons concrete, and
(3) tighten communication practices.
I integrated those by adding a Leader’s Practice Card (daily/weekly habits), clarifying the machine-vs-human
reasoning map, and ensuring each section ends with a “so-what” for real projects.
